Exercise 6.2: Interaction Contrast

Verify that the expression

\[ \tau_{ij} - \tau_{i.} - \tau_{.j} + \tau_{..} \]

is an interaction contrast for the two-way complete model.

  1. Write the list of contrast coefficients in terms of the \(\tau_{ij}\)’s under the following conditions:
    • Factor \(A\) has \(a = 3\) levels.
    • Factor \(B\) has \(b = 4\) levels.

  • Gage bar setting: The distance traveled by the weld die during the automatic weld cycle.
  • Time of welding: The total time of the automatic weld cycle.

Tasks

  1. Using the cell-means model, test the hypothesis that there is no difference in the effects of the treatment combinations on weld strength.

  2. Write contrasts in terms of \(\tau_{ij}\) for:

Exercise 6.12: Memory Experiment

The memory experiment investigates the effects of two factors:

  • Word type: Fruits, nouns, and mixed types.
  • Type of distraction: No distraction, constant distraction, or changing distraction.

The response variable is the number of words remembered, with variance approximated by \(30p(1-p)\).

Task

Determine the number of subjects required to reject the following hypotheses with a power of 0.9 at \(\alpha = 0.05\):

  • \(H_A\): The memorization rate is identical for the three word types.
  • \(H_B\): The distraction types have no effect on memorization.

Assume the minimum difference of interest in memorization rates is 4 words.
