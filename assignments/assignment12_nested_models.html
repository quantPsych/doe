12.3 General Mixed Model

Data Source: Dean et al. (2017), Chapter 12, Exercise 3

Consider the model:

\[ Y_{ijkl} = \mu + \alpha_i + B_j(i) + C_k(ji) + \delta_l + (\alpha\delta)_{il} + (B\delta)_{lj}(i) + \epsilon_{ijkl} \]

Questions

  1. Calculate the expected mean squares for all effects in the model.

  2. Which ratio would you use to test \(H_0: \delta_l + (\alpha\delta)_{il} \, \text{are all equal?}\)

  3. Which ratio would you use to test \(H_0: \sigma^2_\alpha = 0\)?

18.6 Operator Experiment

Data Source: Dean et al. (2017), Chapter 18, Exercise 6

An experiment aimed to determine how much variation in measured manganese concentration in steel is due to operator variability. Ten steel samples were sliced from a billet, and four operators each measured the manganese content twice per sample in a random order.

Questions

  1. Write a model for this experiment. Clearly indicate which effects are fixed, random, crossed, and nested.

  2. Write the degrees of freedom, sums of squares, and expected mean squares for each source of variation.

  3. Investigate whether the normal distribution is a reasonable approximation for the error terms (using the data in Table 18.11). Transformation may be necessary.

  4. If the normal distribution is reasonable, analyze the experiment. Obtain variance estimates for the random effects and identify the major sources of variation.
